**來源：** Rick Warren, *The Purpose Driven Life* (Zondervan, 2002)  
**版本：** PDF, 340 pages, ISBN 0-310-25482-5  
**提取日期：** 2026-04-26

## 一、書籍結構總覽

### 核心框架：40天靈命旅程

> "This is more than a book; it is a guide to a 40-day spiritual journey that will enable you to discover the answer to life's most important question: What on earth am I here for? By the end of this journey you will know God's purpose for your life and will understand the big picture—how all the pieces of your life fit together."
> — 前言 (p.9)

**五大目的（Five Purposes）：**

| 目的 | 標題 | 天數 |
|------|------|------|
| Purpose #1 | You Were Planned for God's Pleasure（上帝的喜悅） | Day 8–14 |
| Purpose #2 | You Were Formed for God's Family（上帝的家庭） | Day 15–21 |
| Purpose #3 | You Were Created to Become Like Christ（像基督） | Day 22–28 |
| Purpose #4 | You Were Shaped for Serving God（服事上帝） | Day 29–35 |
| Purpose #5 | You Were Made for a Mission（使命） | Day 36–40 |

**前置部分（Day 1–7）：** "What on Earth Am I Here For?" — 人生意義的根基問題

**每天的架構（"Thinking about My Purpose"）：**
- **Point to Ponder**：當天核心真理（一句話）
- **Verse to Remember**：聖經金句
- **Question to Consider**：反思問題

## 二、Day 1–7：人生的根基

### Day 1：It All Starts with God

**開篇第一句（全書最著名的句子）：**
> "It's not about you."

**Point to Ponder：** "It's not about me."

**擴展：**
> "The purpose of your life is far greater than your own personal fulfillment, your peace of mind, or even your happiness. It's far greater than your family, your career, or even your wildest dreams and ambitions. If you want to know why you were placed on this planet, you must begin with God. You were born by his purpose and for his purpose."

**邊欄金句（書中側邊語錄格式）：**
> "Focusing on ourselves will never reveal our life's purpose."

> "You were made by God and for God—and until you understand that, life will never make sense."

> "It's in Christ that we find out who we are and what we are living for. Long before we first heard of Christ and got our hopes up, he had his eye on us, had designs on us for glorious living, part of the overall purpose he is working out in everything and everyone." — Ephesians 1:11 (MSG)

**對「自助書籍」的批判：**
> "Self-help books, even Christian ones, usually offer the same predictable steps to finding your life's purpose: Consider your dreams. Clarify your values. Set some goals. Figure out what you are good at. Aim high. Go for it! Be disciplined. Believe you can achieve your goals. Involve others. Never give up. Of course, these recommendations often lead to great success. You can usually succeed in reaching a goal if you put your mind to it. But being successful and fulfilling your life's purpose are not at all the same issue!"

**Verse to Remember：** "Everything got started in him and finds its purpose in him." Colossians 1:16b (MSG)

### Day 2：You Are Not an Accident

> "You are not an accident. Your birth was no mistake or mishap, and your life is no fluke of nature."

> "God prescribed every single detail of your body. He deliberately chose your race, the color of your skin, your hair, and every other feature."

> "While there are illegitimate parents, there are no illegitimate children. Many children are unplanned by their parents, but they are not unplanned by God."

**Point to Ponder：** "I am not an accident."

---

### Day 3：What Drives Your Life?

**Warren 的六大驅動力：**
> "Many people are driven by guilt.... Others are driven by resentment and anger.... Others are driven by fear.... Others are driven by materialism.... Others are driven by the need for approval."

**邊欄金句：**
> "You have just enough time to do God's will. If you can't get it all done, it means you're trying to do more than God intended for you to do."

**專注的力量（預示 Daily Hope「只做最重要的事」）：**
> "Knowing your purpose focuses your life. It concentrates your effort and energy on what's important. You become effective by being selective."

> "If you want your life to have impact, focus it! Stop dabbling. Stop trying to do it all. Do less. Prune away even good activities and do only that which matters most. Never confuse activity with productivity. You can be busy without a purpose, but what's the point?"

> "Without a clear purpose, you will keep changing directions, jobs, relationships, churches, or other externals—hoping each change will settle the confusion or fill the emptiness in your heart."

**上帝的問題：**
> "At the end of your life on earth you will stand before God, and he is going to evaluate how well you served others with your life.... One day God will compare how much time and energy we spent on ourselves compared with what we invested in serving others."

**Point to Ponder：** "Living on purpose is the path to peace."

**Verse to Remember：** "You, Lord, give perfect peace to those who keep their purpose firm and put their trust in you." Isaiah 26:3 (TEV)

### Day 4：Made to Last Forever

> "This life is not all there is. Life on earth is just the dress rehearsal before the real production."

> "Earth is the staging area, the preschool, the tryout for your life in eternity. It is the practice workout before the actual game; the warm-up lap before the race begins. This life is preparation for the next."

> "The most damaging aspect of contemporary living is short-term thinking."

**Point to Ponder：** "There is more to life than just here and now."

## 三、Purpose #1：你是為上帝的喜悅而造的（Day 8–14）

### Day 8：Planned for God's Pleasure

> "You were planned for God's pleasure. The moment you were born into the world, God was there as an unseen witness, smiling at your birth."

> "Bringing enjoyment to God, living for his pleasure, is the first purpose of your life."

> "Bringing pleasure to God is called 'worship.' Anything you do that brings pleasure to God is an act of worship."

> "Worship is a lifestyle."

**邊欄金句：**
> "Worship is far more than music."

> "Worship is not a part of your life; it is your life."

**Point to Ponder：** "I was planned for God's pleasure."

### Day 9：What Makes God Smile?

> "The smile of God is the goal of your life."

**五件讓上帝微笑的事：**
1. When we love him supremely
2. When we trust him completely
3. When we obey him wholeheartedly
4. When we praise and thank him continually
5. When we use our abilities

**Eric Liddell（火戰車）引言：**
> "I believe God made me for a purpose, but he also made me fast, and when I run, I feel God's pleasure."

**關於才能的使用：**
> "You don't bring glory or pleasure to God by hiding your abilities or by trying to be someone else. You only bring him enjoyment by being you."

**Point to Ponder：** "God smiles when I trust him."

### Day 10：The Heart of Worship

> "The heart of worship is surrender."

> "Surrender is not the best way to live; it is the only way to live. Nothing else works."

> "Surrendering to God is not passive resignation, fatalism, or an excuse for laziness... Surrendering is not repressing your personality. God wants to use your unique personality."

> "C. S. Lewis observed, 'The more we let God take us over, the more truly ourselves we become—because he made us.'"

**Point to Ponder：** "The heart of worship is surrender."

### Day 11：Becoming Best Friends with God

> "God wants to be your best friend."

> "Knowing and loving God is our greatest privilege, and being known and loved is God's greatest pleasure."

> "He is a God who is passionate about his relationship with you."

### Day 16：What Matters Most

**全書最重要的主題之一：**
> "Life is all about love."

> "The best use of life is love. The best expression of love is time. The best time to love is now."

**關於時間：**
> "Time is your most precious gift because you only have a set amount of it. You can make more money, but you can't make more time. When you give someone your time, you are giving them a portion of your life that you'll never get back. Your time is your life. That is why the greatest gift you can give someone is your time."

> "Relationships, not achievements or the acquisition of things, are what matters most in life."

> "Busyness is a great enemy of relationships."

> "I have been at the bedside of many people in their final moments... and I have never heard anyone say, 'Bring me my diplomas!... Show me my awards.' What we want around us is people."

> "Wisdom is learning that truth sooner rather than later. Don't wait until you're on your deathbed to figure out that nothing matters more."

**邊欄金句：**
> "The greatest gift you can give someone is your time."

## 五、Purpose #3：你是為了成為像基督（Day 22–28）

### Day 22：Created to Become Like Christ

**全書最重要的原則之一：**
> "You were created to become like Christ."

> "God's ultimate goal for your life on earth is not comfort, but character development. He wants you to grow up spiritually and become like Christ."

> "God is far more interested in what you are than in what you do. We are human beings, not human doings."

**對基督徒誤解的糾正：**
> "Many Christians misinterpret Jesus' promise of the 'abundant life' to mean perfect health, a comfortable lifestyle, constant happiness... In a word, they expect the Christian life to be easy. They expect heaven on earth. This self-absorbed perspective treats God as a genie who simply exists to serve you in your selfish pursuit of personal fulfillment."

> "Never forget that life is not about you! You exist for God's purposes, not vice versa."

**邊欄金句：**
> "God is far more interested in what you are than in what you do."

**Point to Ponder：** "I was created to become like Christ."

---

### Day 23：How We Grow

> "Spiritual growth is not automatic. It takes an intentional commitment. You must want to grow, decide to grow, make an effort to grow, and persist in growing."

> "Nothing shapes your life more than the commitments you choose to make. Your commitments can develop you or they can destroy you, but either way, they will define you. Tell me what you are committed to, and I'll tell you what you will be in twenty years. We become whatever we are committed to."

> "Your character is essentially the sum of your habits."

**改變的方法：**
> "There is a better and easier way: Change your autopilot—the way you think. The Bible says, 'Let God transform you into a new person by changing the way you think.' Your first step in spiritual growth is to start changing the way you think. Change always starts first in your mind. The way you think determines the way you feel, and the way you feel influences the way you act."

**Point to Ponder：** "It is never too late to start growing."

---

### Day 25：Transformed by Trouble

> "God has a purpose behind every problem. He uses circumstances to develop our character."

> "Every problem is a character-building opportunity, and the more difficult it is, the greater the potential for building spiritual muscle and moral fiber."

> "What happens outwardly in your life is not as important as what happens inside you. Your circumstances are temporary, but your character will last forever."

> "You know you are maturing when you begin to see the hand of God in the random, baffling, and seemingly pointless circumstances of life."

> "If you are facing trouble right now, don't ask, 'Why me?' Instead ask, 'What do you want me to learn?'"

**Point to Ponder：** "There is a purpose behind every problem."

## 六、Purpose #4：你是為服事上帝而造的（Day 29–35）

### Day 29：Accepting Your Assignment

> "You were put on earth to make a contribution. You weren't created just to consume resources—to eat, breathe, and take up space."

> "We are only fully alive when we're helping others."

> "Service is the pathway to real significance. It is through ministry that we discover the meaning of our lives."

> "What matters is not the duration of your life, but the donation of it. Not how long you lived, but how you lived."

**有趣的失敗者清單（表明上帝使用各種人）：**
> "Abraham was old, Jacob was insecure, Leah was unattractive, Joseph was abused, Moses stuttered, Gideon was poor, Samson was codependent, Rahab was immoral, David had an affair and all kinds of family problems, Elijah was suicidal, Jeremiah was depressed, Jonah was reluctant, Naomi was a widow, John the Baptist was eccentric to say the least, Peter was impulsive and hot-tempered, Martha worried a lot, the Samaritan woman had several failed marriages, Zacchaeus was unpopular, Thomas had doubts, Paul had poor health, and Timothy was timid. That is quite a variety of misfits, but God used each of them in his service. He will use you, too, if you stop making excuses."

**Point to Ponder：** "Service is not optional."

---

### Day 30–31：Shaped for Serving God / Understanding Your Shape

**SHAPE 首次出現（Day 30，p.235）：**
> "To help you remember five of these factors, I have created a simple acrostic: SHAPE."

**SHAPE 的五個部分：**
> "Whenever God gives us an assignment, he always equips us with what we need to accomplish it. This custom combination of capabilities is called your SHAPE:
> - **S**piritual gifts
> - **H**eart
> - **A**bilities
> - **P**ersonality
> - **E**xperience"

**關於靈命恩賜（Spiritual Gifts）：**
> "You can't earn your spiritual gifts or deserve them—that's why they are called gifts! They are an expression of God's grace to you."

> "An unopened gift is worthless."

> "Two common problems are 'gift-envy' and 'gift-projection.'"

**關於心志（Heart）：**
> "The Bible uses the term heart to describe the bundle of desires, hopes, interests, ambitions, dreams, and affections you have. Your heart represents the source of all your motivations—what you love to do and what you care about most."

> "Another word for heart is passion."

> "When you are doing what you love to do, no one has to motivate you or challenge you or check up on you."

**關於能力（Abilities）：**
> "The average person possesses from 500 to 700 different skills and abilities—far more than you realize."

> "God never wastes abilities; he matches our calling and our capabilities."

> "What I'm able to do, God wants me to do."

**關於性格（Personality）：**
> "There is no 'right' or 'wrong' temperament for ministry. We need all kinds of personalities to balance the church and give it flavor."

> "Woodworkers know that it's easier to work with the grain rather than against it. In the same way, when you are forced to minister in a manner that is 'out of character' for your temperament, it creates tension and discomfort."

> "It feels good to do what God made you to do."

**關於經歷（Experience）：**
> "God intentionally allows you to go through painful experiences to equip you for ministry to others."

> "God never wastes a hurt! In fact, your greatest ministry will most likely come out of your greatest hurt."

> "The very experiences that you have resented or regretted most in life—the ones you've wanted to hide and forget—are the experiences God wants to use to help others. They are your ministry!"

**Day 30 Point to Ponder：** "I was shaped for serving God."

**Day 31 Point to Ponder：** "Nobody else can be me."

---

### Day 32：Using What God Gave You

**如何找到你的 SHAPE：**
> "Begin by assessing your gifts and abilities. Take a long, honest look at what you are good at and what you're not good at."

> "The best way to discover your gifts and abilities is to experiment with different areas of service. I could have taken a hundred gift and ability tests as a young man and would have never discovered that I was gifted at teaching because I had never done it! It was only after I began accepting opportunities to speak that I saw the results, received confirmation from others, and realized, 'God has gifted me to do this!'"

> "Just start serving, experimenting with different ministries, and then you'll discover your gifts. Until you're actually involved in serving, you're not going to know what you're good at."

**比較的陷阱：**
> "Don't compare your shape, ministry, or the results of your ministry with anyone else."

> "The Bible warns us never to compare ourselves with others: 'Do your own work well, and then you will have something to be proud of. But don't compare yourself with others.'"

**Point to Ponder：** "God deserves my best."

---

### Day 33：How Real Servants Act

> "Your shape reveals your ministry, but your servant's heart will reveal your maturity."

> "While knowing your shape is important for serving God, having the heart of a servant is even more important."

**Point to Ponder：** "We serve God by serving others."

## 八、Warren 的修辭模式與標誌性語言

### 1. 開篇震撼式（Shocking Opener）
每章以最短、最有力的宣告開始：
- "It's not about you." (Day 1)
- "You are not an accident." (Day 2)
- "You were planned for God's pleasure." (Day 8)
- "God wants to be your best friend." (Day 11)
- "Life is all about love." (Day 16)
- "You were put on earth to make a contribution." (Day 29)
- "You were made for a mission." (Day 36)

### 2. 邊欄金句（Sidebar Quote）格式
書中每隔幾段就有一個放大的短句在邊欄，這是他「要記住的核心真理」：
> "Focusing on ourselves will never reveal our life's purpose."
> "God is far more interested in what you are than in what you do."
> "Your circumstances are temporary, but your character will last forever."
> "The greatest gift you can give someone is your time."

### 3. 「反轉期望」結構
先說出世俗的假設，再用「But...」或「However...」反轉：
> "You can usually succeed in reaching a goal if you put your mind to it. But being successful and fulfilling your life's purpose are not at all the same issue!"

### 4. 三段對比格式
> "The best use of life is love. The best expression of love is time. The best time to love is now."
> "Through salvation our past has been forgiven, our present is given meaning, and our future is secured."

### 5. 名人語錄作為佐證
每章都引用著名人物（非基督教或基督教）作為真理的「世俗佐證」：
- Bertrand Russell（無神論者）：「如果沒有上帝，生命目的這個問題就毫無意義。」
- George Bernard Shaw：「生命真正的喜悅是被一個你自己承認是偉大目標所利用。」
- C.S. Lewis：「我們越讓上帝接管，就越真正成為自己。」
- A.W. Tozer：「很多人仍在掙扎，是因為他們還沒有到達自我的盡頭。」

### 6. 超具體的練習（Hyper-specific Exercise）
Day 32 的範例（最接近日常靈修格式）：
> "Begin by assessing your gifts and abilities. Take a long, honest look at what you are good at and what you're not good at. Make a list. Ask other people for their candid opinion. Tell them you're searching for the truth, not fishing for a compliment."

> "Make a careful exploration of who you are and the work you have been given, and then sink yourself into that."

### 7. 上帝的「聲音」（Direct Speech）
Warren 經常把上帝說話模擬出來（用引號或意譯）：
> "God said, 'It's time to get on with your life! Do the things I designed humans to do. Make love to your spouse. Have babies. Raise families. Plant crops and eat meals. Be humans! This is what I made you to be!'"

### 8. 「當你……上帝……」的對仗句式
> "When you fully understand this truth, you will never again have a problem with feeling insignificant."
> "When you grasp the eternal consequences of your character development, you'll pray fewer 'Comfort me' prayers and more 'Conform me' prayers."

### 9. 每天的固定結尾格式
每天結束都是：
- **Point to Ponder**（一句核心真理）
- **Verse to Remember**（聖經金句，通常是現代翻譯）
- **Question to Consider**（通常是一個個人應用問題）

## 九、SHAPE 框架的正確用法（書中原意）

**重要：** 在書中，SHAPE 是 Day 30–31 的專門教學章節，不是每次都要展開的公式。

在日常靈修（Daily Hope）中，Warren **從不**逐字母解釋 SHAPE。他的典型用法是：

> "Your SHAPE is a clue to where God wants to use you."（一句帶過）

書中的核心洞見是：
1. 上帝按你的 SHAPE 造你（不是偶然）
2. 你的最大傷痛很可能是你最大的事工
3. 先去服事，在服事中發現你的恩賜（不要等「發現自己」再去服事）
4. 比較是喜樂的竊賊

## 十一、對「Purpose」問題的直接回應模式

**當有人問「我怎麼找到人生目的」，Warren 的典型回應結構：**

1. **入口：** 指出他們的錯誤起點（「你從自己開始找，所以找不到」）
2. **翻轉：** "Start with God, not yourself."
3. **原則：** 上帝在你出生前就計劃好了
4. **工具：** 看你的 SHAPE（一句帶過，不展開）
5. **具體行動：** 開始服事——在服事中發現恩賜
6. **反思問題：** "What do you think God is asking you to do with what you've been through?"

**關鍵洞見（適用於失業、轉型、迷失方向）：**
> "Without a clear purpose, you will keep changing directions, jobs, relationships, churches, or other externals—hoping each change will settle the confusion or fill the emptiness in your heart. You think, Maybe this time it will be different, but it doesn't solve your real problem—a lack of focus and purpose."

> "You have just enough time to do God's will. If you can't get it all done, it means you're trying to do more than God intended for you to do."
